# 🗺️ Changing States — Complete Move Guide

> [!NOTE]
> **This is the companion to the [Apartment Move-In Checklist](./apartment-move-checklist.md).** That checklist still applies in full — utilities, packing, keys, the day-one kit. **This page is only the *extra* stuff that a cross-state move adds:** the deadline-driven, legally-required updates that don't come up when you move across town.
>
> Owner tags match the other doc: **(Both)** = together · **(Each)** = you each do your own copy (licenses, IDs, personal accounts) · **(Either)** = one of you handles a shared item.

> [!WARNING]
> **Several of these are on legal clocks.** Most states give you **10–90 days after establishing residency** to get a new driver's license and re-register your vehicle — and the two often have to happen together. Miss the window and you can face fines or trouble at your next renewal. **Look up your *specific* new state's deadlines the week you arrive** and put them on a shared calendar.

> **New state:** _______  ·  **Residency established (move date):** _______  ·  **License deadline:** _______  ·  **Vehicle registration deadline:** _______

---

## 1. Before you leave the old state

Handle these while you still have easy access to old-state offices, records, and providers.

- [ ] **(Each)** Look up the **new state's residency deadlines** for driver's license, vehicle registration, and voter registration — write them at the top of this doc
- [ ] **(Each)** Gather **proof-of-identity documents** you'll need at the new DMV: birth certificate or passport, Social Security card, and — increasingly — a **REAL ID**-compliant set. Requirements are stricter than a renewal, so check the new state's list now
- [ ] **(Each)** Pull your **driving record / abstract** from the old state if the new state asks for it
- [ ] **(Both)** Request **medical, dental, and vet records** (or confirm they can transfer electronically) before you leave current providers
- [ ] **(Each)** Ask your **doctor for enough prescription refills** to cover the gap until you're set up with a new pharmacy and physician in-state
- [ ] **(Both)** Note your **old state's rules for the final year's taxes** — you'll likely file a **part-year resident return** in *both* states (see §5)
- [ ] **(Each)** Check whether any **professional license, certification, or permit** you hold is state-issued and needs to be transferred or re-applied for (see §4)

---

## 2. Driver's license & state ID

> [!IMPORTANT]
> You generally **cannot hold two state licenses at once.** Getting the new one surrenders the old. Do this *before* the deadline, and bring more documents than you think you need — DMV trips are painful to repeat.

- [ ] **(Each)** Find the new state's rules: some let you **transfer** an out-of-state license without a road test; some require a written/vision test; a few require a road test
- [ ] **(Each)** Bring: current license, **proof of identity**, **proof of the new address** (lease + a utility bill or bank statement — often *two* proofs), and **Social Security** proof
- [ ] **(Each)** Decide **REAL ID vs. standard** — REAL ID is required to fly domestically. If you're going to the DMV anyway, do REAL ID now and bring the extra documents
- [ ] **(Each)** **Surrender the old license** (usually automatic when issued the new one)
- [ ] **(Each)** Update your **license number** anywhere it's on file (auto insurance, employer, TSA PreCheck / Global Entry profile)

---

## 3. Vehicle: registration, title, inspection & insurance

> [!CAUTION]
> **Do these in the right order — insurance usually has to come first.** You typically need an **in-state auto insurance policy** before you can register, and sometimes a **safety and/or emissions inspection** before that. Budget for **title, registration, and possibly a use/sales tax** on the vehicle's value.

- [ ] **(Each)** **Re-shop auto insurance** for the new state and switch your policy to an in-state one (rates and required minimum coverage vary by state)
- [ ] **(Each)** Get any required **safety / emissions inspection** the new state mandates
- [ ] **(Each)** **Re-title and re-register** the vehicle in the new state (bring title, proof of insurance, proof of ID/residency, inspection cert)
- [ ] **(Each)** Get **new license plates** and surrender or dispose of old plates per the old state's rules (some require you to turn them in to cancel registration)
- [ ] **(Each)** Order a **new parking permit / residential zone sticker** if the new area uses them
- [ ] **(Each)** Update the **address on your car loan / lease** if financed
- [ ] **(Both)** If you have a **toll transponder** (E-ZPass, etc.), update the account address / plate — or get the new region's transponder

---

## 4. Voting, taxes-setup & professional licenses

### Voter registration
- [ ] **(Each)** **Register to vote** in the new state (often doable at the DMV during the license step — ask)
- [ ] **(Each)** Cancel or let lapse the **old-state registration** (usually automatic once you register in the new state)

### Professional & other state-issued licenses
- [ ] **(Each)** Transfer or re-apply for any **occupational license** (nursing, teaching, law, real estate, CPA, cosmetology, trades). Some states have reciprocity; some require re-testing — start early, these are slow
- [ ] **(Each)** Update **concealed-carry / firearm permits** — reciprocity and rules differ sharply by state
- [ ] **(Each)** Transfer any **hunting / fishing license** if relevant
- [ ] **(Each)** Re-register to vote and update **jury-duty address** on file

---

## 5. Taxes — the part-year year

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The year you move is the tricky one. You'll usually file a **part-year resident return in both** the old and new states, splitting income by when you lived where. States with **no income tax** (e.g., moving to/from FL, TX, WA, NV, TN) change the math a lot.

- [ ] **(Both)** Update your **address with the IRS** (Form 8822) and state tax agencies
- [ ] **(Both)** Tell **payroll / HR** your new state so **state withholding** switches — otherwise you'll owe or over-withhold to the wrong state
- [ ] **(Both)** Note your **exact move date** — it's the line that splits income between states at tax time
- [ ] **(Both)** If self-employed, update **estimated-tax** payments to the new state
- [ ] **(Each)** Keep receipts — some **moving expenses** are deductible for certain filers (e.g., active-duty military) or on some state returns
- [ ] **(Both)** Flag it for your **accountant** now if you use one — a two-state year is where people get surprised

---

## 6. Banking, insurance & healthcare re-setup

- [ ] **(Each)** Confirm your **bank / credit union** operates in the new state, or open a local account if yours doesn't (some are region-locked)
- [ ] **(Each)** **Health insurance:** confirm your plan's **network covers the new state** — an HMO/EPO tied to the old region may not. A move is a **qualifying life event** that opens a special enrollment window if you need to change plans
- [ ] **(Each)** Find and register with a **new primary-care doctor, dentist, and pharmacy** in-network locally; transfer prescriptions
- [ ] **(Each)** Re-check **life / disability** policies for any state-specific terms
- [ ] **(Both)** Update **renters insurance** to the new state (rates and coverage norms vary)
- [ ] **(Both)** Locate the nearest **hospital, urgent care, and pharmacy** in the new area

---

## 7. Pets, kids & everything-else

- [ ] **(Both)** **Pets:** update the **microchip registry** address, transfer vet records, check the new state/city's **license, rabies, and leash rules**, and re-register the pet locally
- [ ] **(Both)** **Kids:** request **school records / transcripts**, research and enroll in the new school district (proof of residency required), transfer **pediatrician** and immunization records
- [ ] **(Each)** Update address on **passport / immigration documents** if a visa or green card requires reporting a move (e.g., USCIS **Form AR-11 within 10 days** for many non-citizens)
- [ ] **(Both)** Update the state on any **estate documents** — a will, power of attorney, or healthcare directive is state-specific and may need re-execution
- [ ] **(Both)** Research the **new area's basics**: severe-weather risks, recycling/trash rules, transit, and local-government services

---

> [!TIP]
> **Suggested first-two-weeks order after you arrive:** (1) auto insurance → (2) inspection if required → (3) driver's license + voter registration in one DMV trip → (4) vehicle registration & plates → (5) payroll/HR withholding + IRS/state address → (6) new doctor/pharmacy + prescription transfer. Everything else can trickle in. Run the [Apartment Move-In Checklist](./apartment-move-checklist.md) in parallel for the physical move itself.
